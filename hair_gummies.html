Persona × Narrative → Hero / Banner 3.5
The headline 'Biotin Hair Gummies (30N)' is purely product-descriptive. Subhead 'Helps control Hair fall' is generic and doesn't create emotional urgency. Fails to address visible hairfall panic (comb, pillow, shower drain).
Replace headline with: 'Hairfall on your comb, pillow & drain? It's not the water—it's what's missing from your diet.' Persona × Narrative → CTA Language 3.5
CTAs 'Add To Cart' and 'Buy Now' are transactional and generic. They don't reflect the emotional state of someone who discovered a simple fix after failed attempts with nuske and oils.
Primary CTA: 'Start My 90-Day Fix' (action-oriented, timebound). Secondary CTA: 'Get My Gummies' (ownership language). Add urgency microcopy below CTA: 'Hairfall only gets harder to reverse—start today.' This transforms the CTA from transaction to transformation.

Banner includes certifications and generic attributes. The copy 'Typically, visible results may take around 6 months' directly contradicts the narrative's '90-day results' promise.
Remove or reframe the 6-month statement: 'First signs in 6-8 weeks, visible results by 90 days.' Replace certification-heavy banners with myth-busting content: 'Why oils & shampoos failed: They treated outside, not inside' → 'Indian diets lack 40% daily Biotin—one gummy fixes that.' Keep FSSAI badge small for credibility, but lead with narrative.
Visual Design → Lifestyle Shots 4.0
UGC images show product at desk, cafe, with cap and alarm clock—feel incidental rather than aspirational. No images showing persona's actual context: mess food, anxious hair-checking, hostel/PG living, or post-results confidence.
Commission 3-4 new lifestyle shots: (1) Young guy at laptop with gummies next to chai and tiffin—morning routine context (2) College hostel room setup showing gummies as part of daily essentials (3) Before/after confidence shot—anxious mirror check vs. confident social moment (4) 'Mess food + gummies' visual showing how it supplements irregular diet. These make the solution feel achievable.

Proof Prominence
8.0/10
Clinical trial data is prominently displayed in Image 5 with 90% and 80% statistics in large blue circular badges. The CTRI (Clinical Trials Registry India) logo and QR code add credibility. The '90 days' timeframe is repeated across multiple slides. However, the percentages are somewhat buried in text-heavy descriptions. The 'Clinically Proven Ingredients' headline (Image 2) is strong but the actual proof points compete with ingredient descriptions.
Make the key stat '93% saw results' larger and more isolated - consider a dedicated slide with just that number in bold, followed by supporting statistics. Use contrasting colors (gold/yellow) for the percentage numbers against the blue background.
Ingredient Imagery
7.0/10
Image 2 shows ingredients with scattered almonds, strawberries, and seeds around the product - visually appealing and suggests natural/premium quality. The strawberry positioned near the product reinforces the flavor. However, the actual hero ingredients (Biotin, Zinc, Vitamins A/C/E) are only shown as text lists on the packaging and slides, not as recognizable visual elements. The ingredient-to-benefit connection is text-based rather than visual.
Create visual iconography for each key ingredient with micro-imagery (biotin molecule, zinc element, vitamin symbols) paired with benefit arrows. Show ingredient sources more explicitly - eggs for biotin, nuts for zinc - in a dedicated ingredient showcase slide.

Add a powerful before/after slide showing: (1) A comb with significant hair before vs. minimal hair after 90 days, (2) A pillow comparison, or (3) A drain comparison. These visceral, relatable visuals would directly address the persona's panic trigger points.
